First visit

A simple patient journey from first enquiry to next steps.

This page gives patients a practical overview of how to prepare, what may happen at an early appointment, and how to make the conversation with the practice more productive.

Your visit

What the first steps can look like.

01

Reach out

Call or email the practice to ask about appointments, directions, and what to bring.

02

Initial discussion

Share the main concern, whether it is crowding, spacing, bite discomfort, or a general smile review.

03

Assessment and explanation

The visit can include an examination and a clear explanation of findings and possible options.

04

Plan the next step

Patients leave with a clearer idea of timing, follow-up, and how treatment may progress.

Helpful to bring

  • Referral letters, if you were sent by a dentist or doctor.
  • Previous x-rays, records, or dental history if available.
  • A list of current medications, allergies, or medical concerns.
  • Questions about timing, treatment goals, and follow-up.

Helpful to ask

  • What the main concern appears to be.
  • Whether any further records may be needed.
  • What the next step or follow-up could involve.
  • How to stay in touch with the practice after the visit.
